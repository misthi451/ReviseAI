📚 Topic: Photosynthesis · Difficulty: Medium

Key Concepts

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize nutrients from CO₂ and water.
Two main stages: Light-dependent reactions (thylakoid membranes) and Light-independent reactions / Calvin Cycle (stroma).
Chlorophyll pigments absorb red (~700nm) and blue (~430nm) light — green light is reflected, making plants appear green.
Overall equation: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + light energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
ATP and NADPH produced in light reactions are used to power the Calvin Cycle to fix CO₂ into glucose.
Factors affecting rate: light intensity, CO₂ concentration, temperature, water availability.
